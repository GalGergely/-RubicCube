Project Details

The objective of my thesis was to create a Rubik's Cube that could assist users in learning how to solve it blindfolded. The motivation behind the project was to develop a tool as a hobby that combines entertainment and learning while enhancing logical thinking and problem-solving skills.

During the development process, I gained a wealth of fundamental knowledge about the algorithms and solving methods underlying the Rubik's Cube. One of the main goals of the project was to expand my own capabilities and knowledge while creating a tool that could help those who wish to explore the world of Rubik's Cube.

Overall, this project was a personal developmental journey that taught me perseverance, creativity, and the notion that learning and fun can go hand in hand. The creation of the application not only reflects my growth in knowledge and skills but also provides inspiration for others who want to learn and create new things as a hobby.

With this project, my aim is for both complete beginners and players already capable of solving the Rubik's Cube to learn how blindfolded solving works within the world of Rubik's Cube. This program was of interest to me because I had never actually managed to learn this solution. Therefore, I approached the task with the perspective and enthusiasm of an experienced cuber, yet with a layman's view.

About

The Old Pochmann method is a popular method for solving the Rubik's Cube, which combines memorization and two-step algorithms.

The goal of the Old Pochmann method is to solve the cube using simple, easy-to-remember moves that swap corner and edge pieces one at a time into their correct positions.

Alternative Cube Moves

If you are unfamiliar with the fundamental nomenclature of the Rubik's Cube, including the abbreviations for rotation names, we recommend reading the subsequent paragraph. Otherwise, you may proceed to the next section.

The letters designate the movement of the corresponding side of the cube. For instance, rotating the left side is denoted as L. To indicate a reverse rotation, the notation L' is used. This system allows cubers to create concise, easily understood, and universally applicable algorithms.

F: Front, B: Back, L: Left, R: Right, U: Top/Up, D: Bottom/Down

Edge Setups

To accomplish blindfolded cube-solving, it is necessary to assign a unique identifier to each sticker on the cube and recall them later. Typically, people use letters to create words and subsequently form sentences with those words to facilitate memorization. For instance, if the initial letter is "A" and the subsequent one is "L," one might form the word "almost." By remembering the sentence containing these words, the cube can be solved.

The program employs integers rather than characters for this purpose. To facilitate conversion, a reference table is provided. However, it is crucial to note that the cube orientation within the program differs from the conventional one. In this case, the yellow side corresponds to the white, the green and blue sides remain unchanged, but the red and orange sides are also flipped.

It is highly recommended that you become proficient in solving the edges before progressing to this portion of the procedure. During the transition between solving the edges and corners, a unique situation known as "parity" may arise. The parity algorithm is supplied by the program and can also be found in the necessary algorithms menu. Parity occurs when the edges have been solved using an odd number of moves.

Necessary Information

  • The "play" button opens the auto-solving mode of the cube.
  • The "algorithm mode" button opens the learning mode. You can add your own algorithms or learn the pre-programmed ones, if you don't need them anymore, you can delete them later.
  • The "settings" button opens a new page, where you can set the color of the cube and some other settings. You can make the cube stop in every move, or make the Face-IDs show. The facial identifiers serve as distinct markers for each side of a puzzle. Consequently, every individual sticker possesses a unique identification, such as the aforementioned. Within the human-solving approach, these identifiers are denoted by alphabetic characters. For further information, please refer to the sections on corner and edge parity.
  • The "info" button will open this webpage.
  • Your keypresses won't register until the animation is over. For the flawless working of the program and for the best experience, please always wait for the animation to end.
  • You can "ruin" the solving by turning w or m moves, when you shuffle the cube. (clockwise and counter clockwise both)
